Problem Solving

I grew up in Ohio and was born with the skills to solve tough problems. I was raised by loving parents who put an emphasis on hard work and family values.

I honed my problem solving skills at a young age through science fair projects and fixing equipment on the family farm. This helped me discover that I had a fascination for how things work. I knew at an early age I was meant to be an engineer.

I brought my talent, passion, and motivation to the College of Wooster where I earned a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science in 2009. Two years later I made my debut as a software developer in New York City.

In the last 3 years, each project I've worked on has been written in a different language and a different framework and I absolutely enjoy it. I've been lucky to have the opportunity to experience different schools of thought when it comes to solving both common and irregular web problems.

Consulting

For the last 3 years, I have been working as an agile software consultant at Cyrus Innovation where I have the pleasure of teaching clients the value of Agile Software Development and Extreme Programming practices.

My favorite aspects of Agile Software Development are Pair Programming and Test Driven Development.

I like Pair Programming because it just makes work more fun. In my opinion, there is no better way to learn a technology, process, or programmer than when you pair. It's unnatural to spend your whole day in front of a computer and as a human I find fulfillment in my job when I pair program.

I find that TDD helps me to better understand the problem I'm testing before I write any code and it reveals insights into the business and its concerns. It means that I must gain a meaningful understanding of the code I am about to write before I write it and I think that's a powerful thing.

Teamwork

There is nothing more satisfying for me than completing a tough project with an excellent group of people. My working style consists of discipline, banter, and enthusiasm and it has served me well on every team I've worked with.

I am an adaptive developer and I don't let my opinions get in the way of building important business feature. It makes me a solid, well-rounded team member who can contribute in any situation.

I value timeliness, consistency and devotion toward the project and the team. While standup may be a short meeting, I believe in its effectiveness for coordinating team effort so I put a lot of emphasis on arriving on time every day.
